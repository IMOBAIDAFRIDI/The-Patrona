Puppy Care

A full “puppy spa” means giving your dog a complete grooming session from head to paw. This can be done by you, a professional groomer, or even your vet. Grooming is not only about making your puppy look neat and cute but also a way to check if your puppy is healthy.

Bathing

Every 3–4 weeks or only when dirty. Avoid frequent baths to protect skin. Use gentle pet shampoos.

Grooming

Brush your puppy 2–3 times a week. Helps reduce shedding, improves coat health, and builds bonding.

Ear Care

Use vet-approved ear cleaner with cotton balls. Never insert cotton swabs inside the canal to avoid injury.

Eye Care

Check for redness, swelling, or discharge. Clean around eyes gently. Consult vet if any unusual signs appear.

Paws & Nails

Trim nails every 3–4 weeks. Check paw pads and dew claws. Ask a vet/groomer if unsure.
